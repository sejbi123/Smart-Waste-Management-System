# Stakeholders Identification Table

## Smart Waste Management System — ECO Tirana

| Stakeholder                         | Stakeholder Role / Responsibility                                                                                                                                                   | Importance                                                                                                                                                             | Influence                                                                                                                                              | Interests / Positive Impacts                                                                                                                                                          | Concerns                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Waste Management Manager**        | Uses the dashboard daily to monitor operations, approve collection schedules, review analytics, and respond to issues. Directly interacts with all major system modules.            | **HIGH** – Core decision-maker for daily operations. Defines business requirements and validates that the system meets operational goals.                              | **HIGH** – Strategic & Tactical management level. Approves schedules, prioritizes zones, and allocates fleet resources.                                | Needs real-time visibility into bin fill levels, truck locations, and schedule status. The system replaces fragmented manual processes, saving time and reducing errors.              | Data accuracy of simulated sensors may not reflect real conditions. Resistance to changing existing workflows. Fear that automation reduces managerial control.                     |
| IT System Administrator | Manages user accounts, roles, access control, system security, audit logs, and data backup/recovery. Ensures the platform remains operational and secure during working hours. | HIGH – Without proper system administration, authentication, role management, and data integrity cannot be guaranteed. Critical for non-functional requirements. | HIGH – Strategic & Technical level. Controls who can access the system and at what level. Can enforce or restrict system features based on security policy. | A stable, secure, and maintainable platform. Clear documentation and structured role definitions. Reliable backup mechanisms and minimal downtime during operational hours (06:00–22:00). | Complexity of managing multiple user roles across different modules. Integration challenges with ECO Tirana's existing infrastructure. Risk of unauthorized access if role permissions are misconfigured. |
| **Truck Driver**                    | Receives optimized routes on a mobile-friendly view. Marks bins as collected, reports issues (damaged bins, access problems), and confirms route completion.                        | **HIGH** – Front-line user who executes collections. Their feedback on route accuracy and mobile usability is critical for system adoption.                            | **MEDIUM** – Operational level. Executes instructions but can flag route inefficiencies that feed back into route optimization.                        | Clear step-by-step navigation saves time and reduces decision fatigue. Digital reporting replaces paperwork. Fewer wasted trips to empty bins.                                        | Unfamiliarity with mobile app. Fear of being tracked or monitored too closely. Connectivity issues in areas with poor mobile signal.                                                |
| **Field Operator (Bin Technician)** | Maintains physical bins in the field. Logs maintenance activities, updates bin status (damaged, repaired, replaced), and uploads photos of issues via the system.                   | **MEDIUM** – Ensures bin data stays accurate. Maintenance logs are critical for the system's operational reliability.                                                  | **LOW–MEDIUM** – Operational level. Executes maintenance tasks and updates status but does not make strategic decisions.                               | Digital work orders replace paper forms. Photo uploads and status updates are faster than phone calls. Clear task lists improve daily efficiency.                                     | Field conditions (weather, connectivity) may make app use difficult. Extra digital steps may feel like added bureaucracy without adequate training.                                 |
| **Citizen of Tirana**               | Optional end-user who submits reports about full bins, illegal dumping, or damaged infrastructure via a public-facing form. Tracks the status of their report.                      | **MEDIUM** – Provides ground-truth data that supplements simulated sensor readings and increases community engagement and public trust in ECO Tirana.                  | **LOW** – No decision-making authority, but the volume and quality of reports can influence where resources are prioritized.                           | A clean city and faster response to waste problems. Transparent status updates on submitted reports. Easy-to-use reporting form (ideally without requiring an account).               | Privacy concerns regarding submitted location data. Doubt that reports will actually be acted upon. Language barriers if the interface is only in one language.                     |
| **ECO Tirana Management**           | Does not use the system directly day-to-day but reviews exported KPI reports, oversees operational costs, and uses analytics to make strategic service decisions.                   | **HIGH** – The primary client and project sponsor. Defines the overall service goals and success criteria and funds development and deployment.                        | **HIGH** – Strategic level. Approves project scope, budget, and final acceptance. Can mandate adoption across the organization.                        | Cost reduction through optimized routes and reduced fuel waste. Improved service quality and fewer citizen complaints. Data-backed reports to present to the municipality.            | ROI justification for the investment. Risk that simulated data may not translate well to real-world performance. Staff adoption and training costs.                                 |
| **Municipality of Tirana**          | Receives periodic compliance and performance reports exported from the system (PDF/CSV). Does not interact with the platform directly.                                              | **MEDIUM** – Regulatory authority that mandates waste management standards. Compliance reports must satisfy their requirements.                                        | **MEDIUM** – Strategic level. Can impose regulations or penalties that affect how the system must operate or report.                                   | Verified data showing that waste collection meets regulatory standards. Reduced illegal dumping. Improved recycling rates and environmental compliance.                               | Data reliability and auditability of exported reports. Whether the system meets official municipal data standards or integration requirements.                                      |
| **Waste Disposal Facility Manager** | Manages the physical sites where collected waste is deposited (landfills, sorting centers, recycling plants). Receives truck arrival data and tracks deposit volumes by waste type. | **MEDIUM–HIGH** – The system must track where waste goes after collection, not just that it was collected. Facility data is needed for recycling compliance reporting. | **MEDIUM** – Operational & Tactical level. Facility capacity constraints may require schedule changes. Contamination rejections affect recycling KPIs. | Advance notice of incoming trucks to manage facility capacity. Accurate records of deposited waste by type (general, recyclable, organic). Reduction in contaminated recycling loads. | Integration complexity if the facility uses its own systems. Accuracy of waste type classification at deposit. Staff at facilities may require additional training on the platform. |
